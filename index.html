Denver Sports Nation

Born and raised in the shadow of the Rockies, Denver sports aren't just entertainment — they're a way of life. From Mile High Stadium to Ball Arena to the Pepsi Center, these teams have shaped my identity as a fan and a competitor.

01

Denver Broncos

The Denver Broncos are the heartbeat of Colorado football. Founded in 1960, they've given the Mile High City some of the greatest moments in NFL history — from John Elway's miraculous drives to back-to-back Super Bowl championships in 1997 and 1998, and the dominant 2015 Super Bowl 50 run.

As a Denver kid, Sunday afternoons in the fall meant one thing: orange and blue. The energy at Mile High is unlike anything else — 76,000 fans rocking in the thin air of the Rocky Mountains.

  1. Super Bowl Wins
    • Super Bowl XXXII (1998) — Elway's first
    • Super Bowl XXXIII (1999) — Back-to-back
    • Super Bowl 50 (2016) — Manning's farewell
  2. All-Time Greatest Moments
    • "The Drive" — John Elway, 1987 AFC Championship
    • Von Miller's strip sack in Super Bowl 50
    • Peyton Manning's record-breaking 2013 season
  3. Why I'm a Fan
    • Grew up watching games with my family
    • Mile High atmosphere is electric
    • Von Miller was my favorite athlete as a kid

The Sheriff. Came to Denver and immediately broke the all-time TD record (55 TDs in 2013). His final act — a Super Bowl 50 victory — was a perfect ending to a perfect career.

02

Colorado Avalanche

  • Stanley Cup Championships
    1. 1996 — Won the Stanley Cup in their first season in Denver after moving from Quebec
    2. 2001 — Patrick Roy's dominant run
    3. 2022 — Cale Makar & MacKinnon's masterclass
  • Why the Avs Are Special
    1. Cale Makar is the best defenseman in the world. Norris trophy winner in 2022 and 2025 as league's best defenseman
    2. MacKinnon is a generational forward talent. 2024 Hart Trophy winner as League MVP
    3. The 2022 Cup run was one of the most dominant in history

The Colorado Avalanche have been one of the NHL's most storied franchises since arriving in Denver in 1995. Three Stanley Cup championships — including the 2022 title, which many hockey analysts call one of the most dominant Cup runs ever — cement the Avs as a true dynasty.

Watching Nathan MacKinnon skate is a privilege. The man plays at a different speed than everyone else on the ice. And Cale Makar? He might be rewriting what it means to be an offensive defenseman in the modern NHL.

8
Defenseman
Cale Makar

The Norris Trophy winner who plays like a forward. Makar's 2022 Conn Smythe performance was historic — arguably the best postseason by a defenseman in the modern era.

29
Center
Nathan MacKinnon

A perennial Hart Trophy contender and the engine of the Avalanche offense. MacKinnon's speed and skill make him one of the most electrifying players in the NHL today.

92
Left Wing
Gabe Landeskog

The captain. Landeskog led with his heart and his hits, bringing an edge and leadership to Colorado that helped build the culture behind the 2022 championship run.

03

Denver Nuggets

For decades, the Denver Nuggets were a playoff-or-bust team that never quite got over the hump. Then came Nikola Jokic. The 3-time MVP transformed the Nuggets into NBA champions, winning the franchise's first title in 2023 in dominant fashion.

What makes this team special is how they win — unselfishly, creatively, and with Jokic orchestrating one of the most beautiful offenses in NBA history. Jamal Murray's clutch gene and Aaron Gordon's defensive intensity are the perfect complements.

  1. 2023 NBA Championship
    • First title in franchise history
    • Jokic: Finals MVP (27.8 PPG, 14.2 RPG, 8.3 APG)
    • Swept the Lakers in the Western Conference Finals
  2. Jokic's MVP Awards
    • 2021 NBA MVP
    • 2022 NBA MVP
    • 2024 NBA MVP
  3. Why I Love the Nuggets
    • Jokic is the best player in the world
    • Jamal Murray is a playoff beast
    • They brought an NBA championship to Denver
15
Center
Nikola Jokic

The Joker. A 3x MVP who defies every basketball convention — a 7-foot center who is the best passer and playmaker on his team. Arguably the greatest offensive player in NBA history.

27
Guard
Jamal Murray

Ice in his veins. Murray's 2020 bubble playoff run and his 2023 championship performance (including a stunning Game 5 buzzer-beater) proved he's one of the best clutch players alive.

32
Power Forward
Aaron Gordon

The glue. Gordon's defensive versatility and ability to finish at the rim makes him the perfect third piece alongside Jokic and Murray. His motor is relentless every single night.
